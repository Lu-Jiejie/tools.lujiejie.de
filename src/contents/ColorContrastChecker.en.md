## What Is Color Contrast?

[**Color contrast**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contrast_ratio) measures the difference in brightness between a foreground color (such as text or icons) and its background.

Higher contrast generally makes content easier to read. Low contrast can make text difficult to distinguish, especially in bright environments, on lower-quality displays, or for people with visual impairments.

This tool calculates contrast ratios based on the [WCAG](https://www.w3.org/WAI/standards-guidelines/wcag/) accessibility guidelines and checks whether a color combination meets AA or AAA requirements.

## How to Use

- Enter a foreground color and a background color. Hex, RGB, RGBA, HSL, and HSLA formats are supported.
- Use the live preview to see how the colors look in practice.
- Check the contrast ratio and WCAG compliance results.
- If the combination does not pass, use the suggested colors to improve readability.

### Understanding the Results

- **AA** — The minimum recommended accessibility standard for most websites and applications.
- **AAA** — A stricter standard intended for higher accessibility requirements and text-heavy content.
- **Most readable text color** — Whether black or white provides better readability on the selected background.
- **Nearest AA / AAA foreground** — A suggested foreground color that meets the chosen standard while staying as close as possible to the original color.
