## What is the Pomodoro Technique?

The [**Pomodoro Technique**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pomodoro_Technique) is a time management method that breaks work into focused sessions separated by short breaks.

It was developed by Francesco Cirillo in the 1980s and remains one of the most widely used productivity techniques today.

## How It Works

The timer alternates between focus and break sessions.

After each focus session, a short break begins. Once the configured number of sessions has been completed, the next break becomes a long break. The dots below the timer show your progress toward the next long break.

Session lengths and the number of sessions per cycle can be adjusted in the settings.

## Controls

- Start or pause the current session
- Reset the current session
- Skip to the next session
- Enable **Auto Start Next Session** to keep the cycle running automatically

## Shortcuts

- `Space` Start or pause
- `R` Reset
- `S` Skip
- `F` Toggle fullscreen
